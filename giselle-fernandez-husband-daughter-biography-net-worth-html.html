Giselle Fernandez's Bio: Age, Education & Career

Born on 15 May 1961, Giselle Fernandez was raised in East Lost Angeles in the US. She graduated from California State University, Sacramento in 1982 earning B.A. in Journalism.

The 57-year-old anchor started her television career in 1983 at KRDO-TV and KEYT-TV. She joined KTLA as a reporter and weekend anchor in 1985 after being recommended by actor Fess Parker. Giselle eventually moved to CBS-owned WBBM-TV in Chicago and CBS's newly owned station, WCIX in 1989.

Taking her first national news job for CBS News in October 1991, Giselle became a backup anchor and correspondent for the morning, evening, and weekend news broadcasts. She moved to NBC later, anchoring the weekend edition of the Today Show and also filled in for Brian Williams on NBC Nightly News.

During her period of national news coverage, Giselle reported on the unrest in Haiti, the crisis from Cuban immigration, the trial of the conspirators from 1993 World Trade Center bombing, and the 1989 US invasion of Panama among many other major events.

Co-hosting for Access Hollywood from 1996 to 1999, she went to co-host This Week in History on the History Channel and in October 2001, Giselle rejoined KTLA after returning to Los Angeles.
