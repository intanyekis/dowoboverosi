Dolly Parton Gushes Over Beyonc Amid Rumors She Remade &quot;Jolene&quot;

August 15, 2024
blog

Beyoncé has garnered the praises of country music icon Dolly Parton yet again amid rumors that Bey recorded a cover to Parton’s classic single “Jolene.”

In a recent interview with Knox News, Parton responded to a question regarding Beyoncé having possibly recorded a revamped version of Parton’s signature single, which was released in 1973. And according to Parton, the cover may appear on Beyonce‘s forthcoming Act II album.

“Well, I think she has!” the 78-year-old said of Beyoncé remaking one of her songs. “I think she’s recorded ‘Jolene’ and I think it’s probably gonna be on her country album, which I’m very excited about that.” She continued to gush about Knowles, exclaiming “I love her!” while noting that she’s “a beautiful girl and a great singer.”

However, Parton would admit that she’d hadn’t heard the song herself, but that she “thinks” and “hopes” that the rumors of Beyoncé taking on “Jolene” are true. She also revealed that she’s been in contact with both Beyoncé and her mother, Tina Knowles, both of whom are fans of her music.

“We’ve kind of sent messages back and forth through the years. And she and her mother were like fans, and I was always touched that they were fans, and I always thought she was great,” Parton said.

Dolly Parton was among the first members of the country music community to publicly embrace Beyoncé following her announcement of plans to release a country album.

After the Houston native’s single “Texas Hold ‘Em” shot to No. 1 on the Billboard Hot Country chart, Parton spoke glowingly of the 42-year-old while expressed her anticipation for the full project.

“I’m a big fan of Beyoncé and very excited that she’s done a country album,” Parton wrote on social media at the time. “So congratulations on your Billboard Hot Country number one single. Can’t wait to hear the full album!”

Since revealing that her follow-up to Renaissance will consist entirely of country music during a Verizon Super Bowl commercial, Beyoncé has quickly proved that her dominance transcends genre.

In the wake of “Texas Hold ‘Em”s release, she became the first Black woman to top both the Hot Country Songs chart also became the first woman to also reign atop the Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Charts simultaneously.

Beyoncé’s Act II album is scheduled for release on March 29.
